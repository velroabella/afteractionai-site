## PART E — HOW CERTIFICATION WORKS (ADI vs IGDF)

### ADI Accreditation Process

**Who can apply:** Only nonprofit organizations that train and place assistance dogs.

**Process:**
1. Organization must be an ADI Candidate for at least 2 years
2. Must have placed at least 10 assistance dog teams
3. Pre-packet submission (digital Dropbox folder of documents) — due February (re-accreditation) or March (first-time)
4. On-site assessment by trained ADI assessor over several days
5. Assessor interviews staff, clients, volunteers, and board members
6. Reviews paperwork, files, and operational procedures
7. Observes client interactions and dog training methods
8. Inspects facilities for safety, cleanliness, and compliance
9. Evaluated on: humane training methods, health care, facility safety, legal compliance, ethical business practices
10. **Re-accreditation required every 5 years**

**Handler/Dog Team Certification (ADI Public Access Test):**
- Every graduating team must pass the ADI Public Access Test
- 14 testing modules including vehicle exit, heel position, building entry, distraction resistance, and team bond
- Must score 80%+ "yes" answers; certain mandatory items require "yes"
- Any aggressive behavior = automatic elimination
- Administered by the accredited member organization before team graduation

**Current scale:** ~173 accredited organizations worldwide. ~91 in North America.

### IGDF Accreditation Process

**Who can apply:** Guide dog schools/organizations that have graduated at least 2 guide dog teams that have worked for minimum 1 month.

**Process:**
1. Organization applies for IGDF membership
2. IGDF Assessor conducts on-site assessment
3. Assessor evaluates against internationally recognized standards (checklists covering administration, risk management, staff education, dog training/behavior, health/welfare, client services)
4. Report submitted to Accreditation Committee (AC)
5. AC reviews and passes recommendation to IGDF Board
6. Board makes final membership decision
7. **Re-assessment every 5 years** by IGDF Assessor

**Current scale:** 97 members across 35 countries.

### Key Differences

| Feature | ADI | IGDF |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | All assistance dogs (service, hearing, guide, facility) | Guide dogs only |
| Minimum placements to apply | 10 teams | 2 teams |
| Candidate period | 2 years minimum | Varies |
| Re-accreditation cycle | Every 5 years | Every 5 years |
| Geographic reach | Global (~173 members) | Global (97 members, 35 countries) |
| VA benefit recognition | Yes — VA requires ADI or IGDF | Yes — VA requires ADI or IGDF |

### VA Benefits Connection
Dogs trained by ADI or IGDF accredited organizations qualify veterans for the VA Service Dog Veterinary Health Insurance Benefit (VHIB), covering veterinary expenses for the working life of the service dog.
