GI Bill & Education Benefits

The Post-9/11 GI Bill (Chapter 33) covers tuition, housing allowance, and books at approved schools. The Montgomery GI Bill (MGIB) and VR&E (Chapter 31) provide additional paths depending on your service and goals.

Not sure which program fits your situation? The AI navigator can help you compare options based on your service history and goals.

Vocational Rehabilitation & Employment (VR&E)

VR&E (Chapter 31) helps veterans with service-connected disabilities prepare for, find, and maintain suitable employment — or achieve independence in daily living. It can cover education, training, job placement, and more.

Eligibility requires a service-connected disability rating and an employment handicap. A VR&E counselor works with you to build a personalized plan.

Transition Assistance & Career Resources

The Transition Assistance Program (TAP) provides separating service members with resources for employment, education, and civilian life. SkillBridge lets service members gain civilian work experience with approved employers before separation.

USAJOBS connects veterans to federal employment with Veterans' Preference protections. Hire Heroes USA provides free career coaching and resume help.
